Maggie Green How to Design a Successful Quote Tweet

Introduction Challenge for JOUR108, Fall 2022

How to make an awesome ...

Assembly time: 30-60 minutes
Awesome rating: 80% awesome

If you are a designer or interested in design, then creating and posting a Twitter quote would be a great way to start. Following these steps could help you begin to gather an understanding of what it means to be a designer. A basic understanding of how to use these tools can also help you with other projects you may want to pursue in the future, whether it's for creating an advertisement, photoshop, or graphic design.

  • Things You'll Need

  • An electronic device that can run the following apps:

  • Adobe Illustrator

  • Twitter

  • Stock Photos(stocksnap.io)

  1. Find a quote that is meaningful to you.

    You can choose any quote that speaks to you. Whether it makes you laugh or cry the quote should make you feel something.

  2. Find a photo that corresponds with your quote.

    Using Stock Photos(stocksnap.io), find a photo that connects to your quote and is also enteraining for your viewers. You want to draw attention to your quote!

    Once you have choosen your picture you need to make sure you save it to you files. To do this you can double click and then click the option Save As. From here you choose which folder you want to save your image to and don't forget to give it a name before you save.

  3. Open Adobe Illustrator and create a project.

    Using pixels (not points) the dimensions should be 1200x675.

  4. Additional tip to organize your tweet.

    You may find it beneficial to create some layers. You can do this by clicking layers to the right of your screen on Adobe Illustrator. At the bottom there should be a little plus sign in a box that you can click in order to create more layers. You can then designate one layer to the photo that you chose and label it background. You can designate another layer to your quote and label it text. This just helps keep your tweet more organized and can help avoid potential problems.

  5. Time to add your photo!

    From your files go ahead and add your image to Adobe Illustrator. Feel free to mess around with your images until you are happy with its positioning.

  6. Now lets add your quote.

    As you add your quote go ahead and play with the colors and type faces that you use! It is important that you make sure that whatever quote you choose is bold and easy to read..

  7. Go ahead and try out some of the different tools Adobe Illustrator offers

    Adobe Illustrator offers many tools that can potentially help make your tweet more eye catching.I know that these tools can sometimes be confusing so be sure to use this video as a refrence if you run into any trouble.

  8. Exporting Image.

    Once you are happy with your tweet its time to export it. In order to make sure you can easily post your tweet you are going to want to save this file as a PNG

    • How to save Tweet as a PNG.

    • To do this you are going to want to go to the very top left side of your screen and click files

    • Then you are going to scroll down until you find the word export. You will use your clicker to hover over this image until you see 'Export As' pop up. Click on this.

    • Now you just need to decided where you want to save it so that you can easily find it later. Once you do this go ahead and click export.

  9. Create a Twitter caption that is engaging to your audience.

    It would also be a good idea to use hashtags that are relevant to your tweet. Using hashtags on Twitter helps you and others join the conversations, connect with what's happening, and become discoverable.

  10. Upload your Tweet!
